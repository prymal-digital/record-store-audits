✅ What Is Working Well
The genuine strengths this store already has
🧑‍🎤
Compelling owner story. Philippe's "About Me" page is unusually personal and human — 15 years managing Rat Records, hitchhiking to Prince concerts, a French expat's love letter to vinyl. This kind of story builds instant connection and trust that most shops can't buy.
📞
Contact information is clear and complete. Phone number, WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram, email form, physical address and opening hours are all present and easy to find. This is a real advantage over most small shops.
⭐
Strong Google reviews on the selling page. Multiple 5-star reviews with real names, detailed stories and warm language — these do serious trust-building work for people thinking of selling their collection.
📋
Crystal-clear selling process. The "What We Buy / What We Don't Buy" section is honest, detailed and specific. This saves time for everyone and positions Philippe as a knowledgeable professional, not a dodgy dealer.
🎁
Gift cards available. Both e-gift and physical gift cards show commercial thinking beyond the basics. This is a genuinely good revenue opportunity, especially around Christmas and Record Store Day.
🔌
Hi-Fi & DJ gear is a smart second vertical. Partnering with a trusted technician (Mike Hall) to offer fully serviced equipment is a differentiator few local record shops can match — it creates an additional reason to visit.
📸
Active Instagram presence with regular content. Posting flick-through videos four times a week is exactly the right content for this audience — it builds anticipation, showcases stock, and drives repeat visits.
🏪
Physical store clarity. The Deptford High Street address, map, and opening times are clearly listed. For a local shop, this is genuinely important — and it's done well here.
⚠️ What Is Holding This Store Back
Key problems — why they matter and how to fix them
1. The online shop is on a completely different website
TrustHigh Revenue Impact
Why it matters
Clicking "Online Selection" takes visitors away from upsidedownrecords.co.uk and onto upsidedownrecords.square.site — a generic Square storefront with a completely different look and feel. This feels like leaving the shop and entering a warehouse.
Effect on sales
Most visitors will not follow the link. Those who do arrive somewhere that looks unrelated, which destroys the trust built by the main site. Abandoned sessions spike whenever a checkout journey crosses domains.
✅ Recommended fix
Either embed the Square shop as an iframe within the site, or migrate to Shopify — the right platform for a record shop at any stage of growth.
2. Homepage has no clear call to action for buyers
ConversionHigh Revenue Impact
Why it matters
The homepage gives equal weight to buying, selling, hi-fi, cassettes, and gift cards — without telling the visitor what to do first. There is no prominent hero button saying "Browse records for sale" or "Shop now".
Effect on sales
Visitors who don't know exactly what they want will leave. A single strong call to action could double the number of people who click through to the shop.
✅ Recommended fix
Choose one primary goal for the homepage (most likely "buy records online") and make it the first thing visitors see — a large button, above the fold, with a clear label like "Browse Our Collection."
3. No product imagery or browsable catalogue on the main site
Product DiscoveryHigh Revenue Impact
Why it matters
There are no photos of actual records for sale on upsidedownrecords.co.uk. The main buying page is entirely text-based. Record buyers are highly visual — they want to see the sleeves, the condition, the genres available before they commit.
Effect on sales
Without product images, the site cannot create desire or urgency. Visitors have nothing to click on, which means they don't stay long enough to become customers.
✅ Recommended fix
Add a "New Arrivals" section to the homepage with photos of 6–12 current records. Even a simple image grid linked to the shop can dramatically increase click-through rates.
4. No email capture on the homepage
Repeat SalesMedium Revenue Impact
Why it matters
A mailing list signup is mentioned deep in the "Buy" page, but there is no signup form or pop-up on the homepage. The vast majority of visitors will never find it.
Effect on sales
Email is the highest ROI channel in ecommerce. Every visitor who leaves without signing up is a lost future customer. A list of even 500 engaged subscribers could generate significant regular revenue through "new arrivals" emails.
✅ Recommended fix
Add a simple signup section to the homepage footer: "Get first dibs on new arrivals — join the list." Offer an incentive like 10% off the first online purchase or early access to new stock drops.
5. Trust signals are buried — not on the homepage
TrustMedium Revenue Impact
Why it matters
The excellent Google reviews are only visible on the "Sell Your Records" page. A first-time buyer landing on the homepage has no idea how good Philippe is or how many people have had great experiences.
Effect on sales
Without social proof above the fold, new visitors leave before they reach the reviews. This is free credibility that is not being used where it would have the most effect.
✅ Recommended fix
Add 2–3 short customer reviews directly on the homepage, with star ratings visible. A simple "★★★★★ 5.0 on Google — 40+ reviews" banner near the top takes minutes to add and builds immediate trust.
6. No visible returns or shipping policy
Checkout ConfidenceMedium Revenue Impact
Why it matters
Online buyers of second-hand records worry about condition. They want to know: what if it's scratched? Can I return it? How much does postage cost? None of these questions are answered anywhere on the site.
Effect on sales
Shoppers who can't find a returns policy typically abandon before purchasing. Clearly stating "if you're not happy, we'll sort it" removes the biggest fear and increases conversions.
✅ Recommended fix
Create a simple "Buying Online" page that explains: condition grading, delivery costs and times, and the returns process. Link it prominently from the Buy page and the online shop.
7. Spelling and grammar errors undermine professionalism
TrustLow–Medium Impact
Why it matters
Several pages contain typos ("Sell you records", "Familarize", inconsistent capitalisation). While forgivable for a one-person operation, these details signal to new visitors that the shop may not be fully professional.
Effect on sales
First-time buyers who are already uncertain about buying second-hand online may be put off by copy that appears rushed or unpolished.
✅ Recommended fix
Do a single pass through every page using a spellchecker (Grammarly is free). Fix typos and ensure consistent style — capitalise product categories consistently, use full sentences where possible.
8. SEO is basic — missing structured data, page descriptions and blog strategy
FindabilityMedium Revenue Impact
Why it matters
The site has a blog section but it does not appear to be actively used as a traffic driver. There are no visible meta descriptions, schema markup for local business, or targeted content for searches like "buy vinyl records London" or "sell record collection Deptford."
Effect on sales
People searching for a local record shop or to sell a collection are not finding this site organically at scale. This is free traffic being left on the table every day.
✅ Recommended fix
Add Local Business schema to the homepage (this tells Google the shop name, address, hours and phone). Write one short blog post per month targeting a specific search query — e.g. "Best record shops in South East London" or "How to value a vinyl record collection."

Prioritised by impact — ranked from most to least important

Add a strong homepage hero with a single "Buy Records" call to action
Your homepage needs one dominant button above the fold. Choose your primary goal — selling records online — and make that button the first thing people see. Include a striking record sleeve image as the background.

Show product photos on the homepage (New Arrivals grid)
Add a 6-image grid of recently arrived records directly on the homepage. Label each with genre and price. This alone gives visitors a reason to stay, browse, and click through to buy.
🔴 Priority: High 📈 Impact: High 🔧 Effort: Medium
4
Move your best Google reviews to the homepage
Take 2–3 of your strongest reviews and display them prominently on the homepage. Add a "5-star rated on Google" badge near the top. This is free credibility and takes under an hour to implement.
🔴 Priority: High 📈 Impact: High 🔧 Effort: Easy
5
Create a visible email signup with a real incentive
Add "Join the list — get first dibs on new arrivals + 10% off your first online order" to the homepage. Email is your most valuable long-term sales channel. Even 200 subscribers would outperform your current Instagram reach for driving purchases.
🔴 Priority: High 📈 Impact: High 🔧 Effort: Easy
6
Publish a clear "Buying Online" policy page
Write a short page explaining: how you grade record condition (VG, EX, etc.), your delivery costs and timescales, and what happens if a record isn't as described. Link it from the buy page and checkout. This one page can meaningfully increase online conversions.
🟠 Priority: Medium 📈 Impact: High 🔧 Effort: Easy
7
Fix all spelling and grammar errors across the site
Run every page through Grammarly (free) and fix typos like "Sell you records", "Familarize", and inconsistent capitalisation. This takes 30 minutes and immediately raises the professional impression of the shop.
🟠 Priority: Medium 📈 Impact: Medium 🔧 Effort: Easy
8
Add Local Business schema markup for Google
Ask your web developer (or use a free plugin like Rank Math if on WordPress) to add structured data to your homepage. This tells Google your address, phone, and opening hours — helping you appear in local "record shop near me" searches.
🟠 Priority: Medium 📈 Impact: Medium 🔧 Effort: Easy
9
Promote your Instagram flick-throughs more prominently
You post four times a week — that is excellent, consistent content. But your Instagram feed is buried at the bottom of the Buy page. Embed your feed on the homepage with a caption like "New arrivals 4x a week — follow us @upsidedownrecords." Turn those videos into a visit driver.
🟠 Priority: Medium 📈 Impact: Medium 🔧 Effort: Easy

Start a simple monthly blog for SEO traffic
Write one short post per month targeting a real search term: "Best second-hand record shops in South London," "How to value a vinyl record collection," or "What makes a vinyl record valuable in 2026?" Each post brings you closer to page 1 for buyers in your area.

🚀 Bigger Growth Opportunities
5 strategic moves with serious long-term impact
🛍️
Migrate to a proper ecommerce platform
Moving from Square to Shopify keeps everything under your brand, enables proper SEO on product pages, allows abandoned cart emails, and gives you far more control over the customer experience. Shopify is built for exactly this — a record shop that wants to grow. This is the single biggest growth lever available.
📬
Build an email list and send weekly "New Arrivals" drops
Record collectors are exactly the kind of audience that responds to email. A weekly "what just came in" email with photos and prices can drive consistent repeat sales from people who already love what you do — with zero advertising spend.
🗺️
Own "second-hand records London" in Google Search
A focused SEO strategy — blog content, local citations, schema markup, and Google Business Profile optimisation — could bring you 500–2,000 extra monthly visitors searching for exactly what you sell. This is compounding, free traffic that grows over time.
🎵
Launch a "Record of the Month" club or subscription
Philippe's expert curation is genuinely rare. A monthly curated subscription box — one hand-picked record per month, with liner notes from Philippe — would command a premium price and create predictable, recurring revenue. This is a natural extension of the personal story.
🤝
Partner with local venues, cafés and labels for cross-promotion
Deptford has a thriving creative community. Partnerships with local venues, artists, or music nights — each tagging @upsidedownrecords and linking to the site — can grow awareness faster than paid advertising at a fraction of the cost. Record Store Day is the obvious annual anchor event.
